FAQ

  • Who can attend in person?
    WPI students can attend the event in-person in the top floor of the Rubin Campus Center. External participants (e.g. participants from other schools, states, etc.) can only participate in Hack@WPI 2022 virtually due to COVID-19 restrictions.
  • Do I need to know how to code in order to participate?
    You do not need to know how to code in order to participate! Hack@WPI 2022 is open to all kinds of STEM majors, and we encourage both software and hardware hacks. This means the event isn't limited to just CS majors or programmers -- practically anybody can participate.
  • Will hardware and tools be offered?
    Tools, hardware and machines are available to WPI students in the Makerspace in the Innovation Studio; students should see makerspace.wpi.edu for more details.

    Virtual participants will need to find their own tools, hardware, and machines to utilize for the hackathon.
  • How big can the teams be?
    Teams can range anywhere from 1 to 5 people.
  • What if I'm a WPI student living off-campus and wish to participate the event overnight?
    Due to updated COVID-19 protocols, we are no longer able to provide food for the event.
  • Will there be food or refreshments?
    Due to updated COVID-19 protocols, we are no longer able to provide food at the event - we are currently looking into alternatives and will be providing additional information prior to the start of the event

    There will also be snacks, water, and other refreshments provided throughout the event. We will also be adhering to food-specific COVID-19 guidelines to ensure everyone's safety.
